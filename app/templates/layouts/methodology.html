Under the hood of Post-Markem

Methodology Note: Alignment ≠ Truth

Post-Markem measures mathematical deviation, not artistic quality. If a couple dances poorly but the entire panel marks them 1st, a judge marking them 6th (justly) will appear "biased" in our stats. We detect outliers relative to the consensus, not absolute truth.

Panel Alignment

How often does a judge agree with the majority? We calculate the Consensus Line using Pearson correlation coefficients.

These are displayed as percentages for clarity. A judge with 90%+ alignment is considered highly aligned with the panel, while 70-80% often indicates independent thinking preserving competance. Everything below 60% may warrant a closer look for potential bias or outlier behavior.

Note: A negative percentage indicates inverted marking (e.g., placing the winner last). Scores below 0% are extreme outliers and suggest a significant deviation from the panel's reality potentionally indicating sabotage.

Voting Blocs

We identify "blocs" by analyzing pairwise correlations between judges. Groups with a correlation above 0.85 are flagged as a voting bloc.

*Correlation does not imply causation. Competent judges often agree naturally, which leads to creation of a voting blocs of highly competent judges. This tool simply highlights consistent patterns of agreement.

Bias Detection

For each adjudicator, we calculate the deviation from the final result per couple.

  • Favoritism: Marking a couple significantly higher than the consensus (Positive deviation).
  • Neutralism: We do not flag adjudicators with deviation between 0.2 and -0.2 to account for the subjectivity of dancing as an art.
  • Harshness: Marking a couple significantly lower than the consensus (Negative deviation).

We visualize the average deviation per judge and breakdown specific dances (e.g., W, T, V, F, Q) at the bottom of each card as well.
